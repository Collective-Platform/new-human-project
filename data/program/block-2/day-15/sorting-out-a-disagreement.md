## Today's Focus

Conflict in the new humanity is real and it must be dealt with directly and graciously.

## Reading Notes

Paul names names. Euodia and Syntyche are two real women and leaders in the church who are not getting along. Paul does not hide this or pretend it isn't happening. He begs each of them personally to make peace, and he asks a trusted friend to help them. This is what healthy conflict resolution looks like in the new humanity. We face problems instead of avoiding them. We talk to people instead of gossiping about them. We get help from others instead of letting things get worse. Paul reminds everyone that both women have worked hard alongside him to spread the gospel. Their disagreement is painful precisely because they matter so much. The new humanity takes relationships seriously enough to do the hard work of making peace.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity does not pretend conflict isn't happening. It names it, addresses it, and seeks reconciliation because the relationships are too important to abandon._

## Reflection

Is there a relationship in your church that is strained or broken? What would it look like to take one step toward addressing it this week?

## Today's Practice

If there is someone you are in conflict with or estranged from, take one concrete step toward reconciliation today even if it is only asking God to give you the willingness.
