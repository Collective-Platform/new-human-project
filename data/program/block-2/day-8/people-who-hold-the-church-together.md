## Today's Focus

What does faithful, unglamorous service in the new humanity actually look like?

## Reading Notes

Paul pauses to commend two people, Timothy and Epaphroditus. Neither is famous. Timothy is described as genuinely concerned for the welfare of others. Paul notes this is a rare quality in a world where most people seek their own interests. Epaphroditus nearly died in service to the church and to Paul. These are not celebrities, but rather the kind of people who hold communities together through faithful, costly, and often invisible work. The new humanity needs people like this more than it needs impressive leaders. And Paul's instruction is pointed: we must honor such people and never take them for granted. The health of the new humanity depends on how it treats those who serve quietly.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity is held together not by the visible and impressive, but by the faithful and the unnoticed. Honour them._

## Reflection

Who are the Timothy and Epaphroditus figures in your church genuinely other-centred, faithful, quietly costly? Have you told them recently what they mean to you?

## Today's Practice

Identify one person in your church who serves faithfully and is rarely thanked. Find a specific, personal way to honour them this week.
