## Today's Focus

The new humanity works out its salvation together, and in doing so, it shines in a crooked world.

## Reading Notes

Work out your salvation, but do not work for it. The salvation is given. Now live it out, together, with seriousness and trust. And the context is clear: a crooked and twisted generation. The world is full of complaint, division, and self-seeking. Into that world, the new humanity is called to shine like stars. They do this not by being impressive, but by being different, all while holding out the word of life. Paul says he will be glad even if he is poured out as a drink offering over their faith. His joy does not depend on his comfort. It depends on whether the new humanity is shining. And he calls them to share that joy with him.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity shines not by performing, but by simply being different in a world that has lost its way._

## Reflection

In your daily life, whether at work, with neighbors, or around your family - what does it look like for your community to bring light into dark places?

## Today's Practice

As a small group or as individuals: identify one concrete, visible way your church community could be a light in your local area this month. Take the first step.
