## Today's Focus

Joy in the new humanity is a command, not a feeling. That is exactly what makes it possible even now.

## Reading Notes

Rejoice in the Lord always. Paul says it twice just in case you missed it. This is not a command to feel happy. It is a command to find your joy in a place where it cannot be stolen. Your joy is found in the Lord, not in your circumstances. The phrase "the Lord is near" gives this joy its foundation. Jesus is close to us right now, and he is returning soon. Because of this, Paul gives us a remarkable instruction: let your gentleness be clear to everyone. True joy changes your character. It creates people who are not driven by anxiety, not grasping for control, and not harsh under pressure. The new humanity is marked by a deep, gentle joy that the world simply cannot duplicate.

## Key Idea

_Joy in the new humanity is not a feeling that comes and goes. It is a settled choice to find your life in Christ. Choosing this joy produces a gentleness that the world will notice._

## Reflection

When life is hard, where do you tend to look for joy? What would it look like to deliberately locate your joy in the Lord rather than in your circumstances?

## Today's Practice

For the rest of this week, every morning before anything else, say out loud: 'I choose to rejoice in the Lord today.' Notice what it does to the rest of your day.
