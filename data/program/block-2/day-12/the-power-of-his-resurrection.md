## Today's Focus

Knowing Christ means sharing in both his resurrection power and his suffering.

## Reading Notes

Paul says something that surprises many people. He wants to know both the power of Jesus' resurrection and the sharing of his sufferings. He wants both, not one without the other. Resurrection power without the cross leads to a false hope, where we think following Jesus means nothing bad will ever happen. But suffering without resurrection leads to complete despair. The new humanity holds both together. There is real power available to us, which is the same power that raised Jesus from the dead. And there is a real cost, because our lives will look like his. This is not a sad bargain. It is the deepest kind of life because it is real, tested, and ultimately undefeated.

## Key Idea

_Knowing Christ means entering both his power and his cross. The new humanity does not choose between them._

## Reflection

Do you tend to expect an easy life, or do you get resigned to hard times without expecting God to move?

## Today's Practice

In prayer today, bring one area of your life where you need resurrection power, and one area where you are being asked to share in Christ's sufferings. Hold both before God.
