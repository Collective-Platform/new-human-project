## Today's Focus

Unity in the new humanity is not sameness, but rather a shared direction of the heart.

## Reading Notes

Paul's appeal here is one of the most tender in all his letters. If there is any encouragement in Christ, any comfort from love, or any fellowship of the Spirit, then complete my joy by being of one mind. He is not asking people to agree on everything. He is asking them to share a posture of humility, being other-centred, and looking to the interests of others rather than only your own. The antidote to division in the new humanity is not a policy or a program. It is people choosing to count others more significant than themselves. This is not weakness. It is the posture of Jesus himself, which Paul is about to describe.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity becomes one not by enforcing agreement, but by every member choosing to put others first._

## Reflection

In what area of your church life are you most tempted to insist on your own way or your own preferences? What would it look like to hold that more loosely?

## Today's Practice

Today, in one specific situation, actively look for the interest of another person before your own. Do it without announcing it.
