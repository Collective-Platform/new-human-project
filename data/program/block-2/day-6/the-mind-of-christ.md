## Today's Focus

The most powerful person in the universe became a servant. That changes everything.

## Reading Notes

This is one of the most breathtaking passages in the New Testament, which serves as a hymn to the humility of Christ. He was in the form of God. He did not cling to that status. He emptied himself, took the form of a servant, became human, and became obedient even to the point of death on a cross. And then God exalted him. The pattern is clear: the path through humility leads to glory. This is the mind Paul calls the new humanity to adopt. This isn't about giving up; it's about being truly free. Because your security doesn't come from status or recognition, you don't have to protect it. Jesus had all the security in the universe, yet he laid it down. Now, the new humanity is called to do the same.

## Key Idea

The mind of Christ is not a mood. It is a deliberate choice to release your grip on status and serve, because your security is already settled.

## Reflection

Where in your life are you clinging to status, recognition, or being right? What would it look like to release that grip this week?

## Today's Practice

Read Philippians 2:5-11 slowly, out loud. Then ask God: 'Where do you want me to have this mind today?' Listen, and respond to what comes.
