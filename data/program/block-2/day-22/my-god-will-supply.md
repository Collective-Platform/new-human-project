## Today's Focus

The promise behind all Christian generosity: God does not let his people run empty.

## Reading Notes

And my God will supply every need of yours according to his riches in glory in Christ Jesus. This is the promise that makes generosity possible in the new humanity. Not every want, every need. And the supply is not from a limited human budget, but from the riches of God in Christ. The Philippians gave generously from what they had. Paul's response is not simply: thank you, that was kind. It is: now watch what God does for you. Generosity in the new humanity is not a drain on the giver. It is the triggering of a promise. The community that gives liberally trusts that the same God who prompted the giving will sustain the giver.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity can give generously because its supply does not depend on what it holds back. God supplies from his own riches._

## Reflection

What is the thing you find hardest to be generous with money, time, energy, recognition? What promise from this verse speaks to that specific reluctance?

## Today's Practice

Pray Philippians 4:19 as a community prayer this week either in a group or personally on behalf of your church. Ask God to be the supply for every real need you are aware of.
