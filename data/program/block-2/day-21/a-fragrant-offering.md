## Today's Focus

The generosity of the new humanity is an act of worship, offered to God through others.

## Reading Notes

Paul uses striking temple language: the Philippians' gift is a fragrant offering, an acceptable sacrifice, pleasing to God. They thought they were sending money to a person. Paul tells them they were offering worship to God. This reframes everything. The new humanity does not divide life into the spiritual and the secular. Every act of generosity, a financial gift, a meal delivered, a car offered, an hour given; is an act of worship when it flows from love for Christ. When you give to a person in need within the community of the new humanity, you are placing an offering before God. That is the dignity Paul ascribes to the Philippians' gift.

## Key Idea

_Every act of generosity in the new humanity is an act of worship. God receives what you give to others as an offering placed before him._

## Reflection

How would it change the way you give if you genuinely believed that your gift was being received by God as worship?

## Today's Practice

The next time you give, money, time, help do it with a conscious prayer: 'Lord, I am offering this to you.' Notice if it changes anything.
