## Today's Focus

What does it look like to live out what you actually believe?

## Reading Notes

Paul uses one key word that ties Philippians directly to Ephesians: "conduct yourselves in a manner worthy of the gospel." Your manner of life should match your message. And this worthy life has a specific shape: standing firm together, striving together as one, and not being frightened by opponents. Paul is describing a congregation that faces pressure as a unit. It is not every individual fighting their own battle, but instead, the new humanity facing resistance shoulder to shoulder. Suffering, he says, is part of the calling. This is not a sign that something has gone wrong, but a sign that the New Human Project is making contact with reality.

## Key Idea

Living worthy of the gospel is not about being impressive. It is about standing firm together as one, even when it costs something.

## Reflection

When your church faces criticism or pressure from the culture around it, do you tend to scatter or stand together? What would standing firm together look like?

## Today's Practice

Identify one way your community's values create friction with the culture around you. Talk about it openly with someone this week so you don't have to face it alone.
