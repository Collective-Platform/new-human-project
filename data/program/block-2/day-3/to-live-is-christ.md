## Today's Focus

What changes when Christ becomes the center of both your life and your death?

## Reading Notes

Paul is facing the real possibility of execution, and he does something most people would find impossible. He refuses to be anxious about it. His confidence is not that he will be released, but that whether he lives or dies, Christ will be honoured in his body. To live is Christ. To die is gain. He is genuinely torn because both outcomes are good, since both are about Jesus. This is not a death wish. It is freedom. The person whose life is defined by Christ is not held hostage by the fear of death or the craving for comfort. People of the new humanity have been given this kind of freedom. Their ultimate wellbeing is secured regardless of what happens to them.

## Key Idea

_When Christ is your life, neither death nor difficulty has the last word. That is the freedom the new humanity lives from._

## Reflection

What are you currently holding onto most tightly? What would it cost you to say 'to live is Christ, to die is gain’ about that area of your life?

## Today's Practice

Pray Philippians 1:20 as your own prayer today. Ask God to be honoured in your body especially in how you speak, work, and treat people. Live this out, whatever happens today.
