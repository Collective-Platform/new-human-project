## Today's Focus

Paul had every religious credential available, yet he counted them all as loss.

## Reading Notes

If anyone had the right to brag about religious credentials, it was Paul. He had it all: the perfect lineage, the highest religious status, and a flawless record. These were real achievements. But when he met Jesus, everything changed. Paul doesn't say his old life was worthless. He just says that compared to Christ, it is a total loss. This isn't about putting himself down; it is just a matter of perspective. When you find something of ultimate value, everything else loses its value. The new humanity is made up of people who have made this exact same exchange.

## Key Idea

_Coming to Christ is not about adding Jesus to your life. It is about recognising that everything else is small compared to him._

## Reflection

What credentials, achievements, or identities have you found hardest to hold loosely since becoming a Christian?

## Today's Practice

Write down three things you tend to base your identity on. Hold each one up to the light of Philippians 3:7-8 and ask: am I treating this as gain or loss compared to Christ?
