## Today's Focus

The new humanity is honest about its imperfection and keeps moving forward anyway.

## Reading Notes

Paul refuses to claim he has arrived. Not that I have already obtained this or am already perfect but I press on. He forgets what is behind and strains forward to what is ahead. This is the healthy posture of the new humanity: not complacency (I'm fine as I am), not shame (I'll never be good enough), but honest, forward-moving pursuit. The past, whether failures or achievements, is released. The prize ahead is Christ himself. Churches that are healthy have this same posture: honest about their imperfection, moving forward together, not paralysed by past mistakes or inflated by past successes.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity is not perfect but it is pressing on. That honest, forward movement is exactly where Christ meets us._

## Reflection

Is there something from your past, a failure, a wound, or a former glory that is keeping you from pressing on? What would it look like to release it?

## Today's Practice

Write down one thing from your past you need to let go of in order to press on. Pray over it. Then literally turn the page, start tomorrow with the posture of someone straining forward.
