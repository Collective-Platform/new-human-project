## Today's Focus

The letter ends where the gospel always begins and ends: grace.

## Reading Notes

The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Four words close the letter: grace, Lord, Jesus, Christ. Everything Paul has written, whether it's the joy from prison, the mind of Christ, the peace that passes understanding, the contentment in all things, the generosity of the community all of it flows from this one reality: the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ. The new humanity is not a community held together by effort, willpower, or good intentions. It is a community continuously sustained by grace. This is where it started in Ephesians, and this is where Philippians ends. Grace is not the beginning of the Christian life. It is the whole of it.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity begins in grace, is sustained by grace, and ends in grace. There is no stage of the journey where you graduate beyond needing it._

## Reflection

As you look back over these 25 days and over your 25 days in Ephesians before them where have you experienced the grace of the Lord Jesus Christ most tangibly?

## Today's Practice

Write a brief letter to your own church community even if you never send it, describing what you have seen of grace in them over the course of this study. Be specific.
