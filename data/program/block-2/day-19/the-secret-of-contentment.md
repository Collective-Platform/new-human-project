## Today's Focus

Contentment is not a personality trait. It is a learned practice and the new humanity can learn it.

## Reading Notes

Paul writes that he has learned to be content in every situation. This is a crucial detail. Contentment is not a natural talent that some people just happen to have. It is a discipline that you develop through experience and trust tested over time. Paul has known both wealth and poverty. He has had plenty, and he has been completely broke. Through all of it, he learned that Jesus is enough in every situation. The famous verse "I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me" is not a promise of superpowers. It is a personal testimony. Paul is saying that Jesus gives him the strength to be content in every circumstance. The new humanity is like a school where we learn this deep contentment together.

## Key Idea

_Contentment is not a natural temperament. It is a hard-won, Spirit-taught practice and the new humanity grows into it together._

## Reflection

In what circumstances do you find contentment hardest? What would it look like to bring those specific circumstances to Christ and ask for his sufficiency?

## Today's Practice

Pick one area of your life where you are consistently discontent. Every time that discontentment surfaces this week, pray: 'Christ is my sufficiency here.' Track whether it begins to shift.
