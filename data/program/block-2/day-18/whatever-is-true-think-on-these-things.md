## Today's Focus

The new humanity deliberately chooses what it thinks about and that shapes everything.

## Reading Notes

Paul gives the new humanity a filter for the mind: whatever is true, honourable, just, pure, lovely, commendable, think about these things. This is not escapism or denial of reality. It is a deliberate choice about where to direct the attention. The mind is not a neutral observer as it is shaped by what it dwells on. A congregation that feeds on criticism, fear, and complaint will become critical, fearful, and complaining. A congregation that deliberately meditates on what is true and lovely and good will be formed in those qualities. What the new humanity thinks about together shapes what it becomes.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity does not let its mind drift to wherever the current pulls. It deliberately directs its attention toward what is true, good, and beautiful._

## Reflection

What do you tend to dwell on most? The failures and frustrations, or the true and the good? What practical changes would help you think differently?

## Today's Practice

Before you go to sleep tonight, spend five minutes deliberately thinking about things that are true, honourable, and lovely. Notice how it changes your state of mind.
