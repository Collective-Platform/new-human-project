## The Condition of Your Heart

## Scripture:

_A good man brings good things out of the good stored up in his heart, and an evil man brings evil things out of the evil stored up in his heart. For the mouth speaks what the heart is full of._ \- Luke 6:45

Most people spend their lives managing appearances.

We learn how to say the right things. We learn how to behave appropriately. We learn how to hide our struggles. Yet Jesus consistently draws our attention beneath the surface.

When He speaks about anger, lust, greed, anxiety, forgiveness, and love, He rarely focuses only on behaviour. He goes deeper. He speaks about the condition of the heart.

The heart, in Scripture, is more than emotions. It is the center of who we are. It is where our desires, motives, fears, hopes, and affections live.

Jesus reminds us that whatever fills the heart eventually overflows into our lives.

Emotional maturity begins when we stop blaming circumstances for everything and start paying attention to our hearts.

This is not an invitation to become self-absorbed. It is an invitation to become self-aware. God is not merely interested in changing what people see.

He desires to transform who we are becoming.

Today, begin by paying attention. Not with judgment. Not with shame. But with curiosity and openness before God.

Ask Him to show you what is truly happening within. The goal is not perfection. The goal is honesty.
