## How to Use This Study Plan

In Ephesians, we saw the blueprint. The New Human Project was laid out in full: who we are in Christ, what community he has created, how our identity reshapes our daily living. Philippians is that same vision tested by real life. Paul writes not from a comfortable study but from a Roman prison and from that cell, surrounded by chains, he overflows with joy. This is what the new humanity looks like under pressure.

Over 25 days, we will discover that the joy, peace, humility, and generosity Paul describes are not personality traits that some people happen to have. They are practices that the new humanity learns together through shared experience, honest reflection, and the continuous grace of Christ.

**Each day includes:**

- **Today's Focus** — A single clear question to anchor your reading.
- **Reading Notes** — Background and insight to help the passage come alive.
- **Key Idea** — One sentence that captures the heart of the day.
- **Reflection** — A question to sit with honestly alone or in a group.
- **Today's Practice** — Something concrete to do, not just think about.
