## Today's Focus

How do we stop just attending church, and start building it together?

## Reading Notes

Paul's opening is instantly warm. He addresses the whole community, the overseers, deacons and the first thing he does is thank God for them. Not for their program, not for their growth statistics, but for their partnership in the gospel from the very first day. The Greek word _koinonia_ means something deeper than attendance or membership. It means shared participation, deep sacrifice, and carrying the weight together. Then Paul prays, and what he asks for is surprising. He does not ask for bigger crowds or better programs. Instead, he prays for love that grows in knowledge and depth, creating people of wisdom and pure character. This is the new humanity, living not as an organization to be managed, but as a family growing in love together.

## Key Idea

_The new humanity is not a crowd attending the same event. It is a true partnership where people are genuinely invested in each other and in the gospel together._

## Reflection

What would _koinonia_, a deep partnership in the gospel, look like in your church this week? How can you move beyond just attending a service?

## Today's Practice

Write down the name of one person in your church whose partnership in the gospel you are genuinely thankful for. Send them a message or tell them in person this week.
