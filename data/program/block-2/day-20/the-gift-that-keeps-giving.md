## Today's Focus

Generosity in the new humanity is not a transaction. It is an expression of love.

## Reading Notes

Paul is careful here. He does not want to appear to be asking for more money. He goes out of his way to say he has learned contentment regardless. But he wants them to know he is glad and genuinely glad not primarily because he received the gift, but because of what giving it did for them. Their generosity is an expression of their love, and love like that produces fruit that accumulates to their account. The new humanity gives not to get something back, not to appear generous, not even primarily because it meets a need but because giving is an expression of the life they have received in Christ. Generosity flows from gratitude.

## Key Idea

_Generosity in the new humanity is not a transaction or a duty. It is love made visible and it does something in the giver as well as the receiver._

## Reflection

When you give financially, with your time, with your energy, what is your primary motivation? Love, duty, guilt, or something else?

## Today's Practice

Do one act of generosity this week that no one will see or know about. Do it for love alone.
