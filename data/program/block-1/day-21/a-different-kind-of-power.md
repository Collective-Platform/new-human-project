## Today's Focus

The New Human Project changes how power works in every relationship.

## Reading Notes

Before Paul says anything about husbands and wives, he lays down the foundation in verse 21: submit to one another out of reverence for Christ. That's not a throwaway line. It's the heading over everything that follows. Mutual submission is the governing principle. Everyone, not just one party in the relationship.

He's not handing men a power card. He's actually doing the opposite. He's calling husbands to model their love on Jesus, who served the church, gave himself up for the church, and put the church's wellbeing ahead of his own comfort. That is the standard Paul sets. Not "you're in charge, so act like it." But "love the way Jesus loved, which cost him everything."

In a world where authority meant power over other people, that was a complete reframe. Authority in the new humanity doesn't look like demanding your rights. It looks like self-giving love. The question you're supposed to be asking isn't "what can I get here?" It's "how can I serve?"

## Key Idea

The New Human Project doesn't give anyone permission to dominate. It calls everyone, especially the powerful to serve.

## Reflection

In your closest relationships, do you tend to lead with your rights or with your responsibility to love? Be honest.

## Today's Practice

In one relationship this week, look for an opportunity to serve before you seek to be served.
