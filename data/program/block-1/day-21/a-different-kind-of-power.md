## Today's Focus

The New Human Project changes how power works in every relationship.

## Reading Notes

Verse 21 is the heading over everything that follows: 'Submit to one another out of reverence for Christ.' Mutual submission is the governing principle of all relationships in the new humanity. When Paul talks about husbands and wives, he's not handing men power — he's calling them to a pattern of love modelled on Jesus, who served and gave himself for the church. That's a far cry from domination. In a world where authority meant power over others, the New Human Project reframes it completely. Authority in the new humanity looks like self-giving love. The question is never 'what can I demand?' but 'how can I serve?'

## Key Idea

The New Human Project doesn't give anyone permission to dominate. It calls everyone — especially the powerful — to serve.

## Reflection

In your closest relationships, do you tend to lead with your rights or with your responsibility to love? Be honest.

## Today's Practice

In one relationship this week, look for an opportunity to serve before you seek to be served.
