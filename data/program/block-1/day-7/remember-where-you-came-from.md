## Today's Focus

Why Paul tells us to remember our past and why that matters.

## Reading Notes

Paul tells us to remember. Not to celebrate something good. He tells us to remember what life was like before Christ: cut off from God's people. A stranger to his promises. No hope. No God. Five negatives in two verses. He does it on purpose.

This isn't about guilt. Paul isn't trying to make you feel bad about who you used to be. He's making sure you feel the full weight of what God has actually done.

You can't fully appreciate the rescue if you forget what you were rescued from. And you can't fully understand why the New Human Project is so remarkable if you forget the darkness it breaks into. Grace always looks biggest when you hold it next to what it replaced.

So remember. Not to feel bad, but to feel grateful. Not to stay stuck in the past, but to stay amazed by what God has done.

## Key Idea

Remembering where we came from keeps us humble, grateful, and full of wonder at what God has done.

## Reflection

What might you start taking for granted about your faith if you forget where you came from?

## Today's Practice

Spend time today in genuine gratitude not just for what God has given you, but for what he brought you out of.
