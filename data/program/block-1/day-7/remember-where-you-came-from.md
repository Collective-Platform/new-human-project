## Today's Focus

Why Paul tells us to remember our past — and why that matters.

## Reading Notes

Paul says: remember. Remember what life was like without Christ — cut off from God's people, strangers to his promises, without hope, without God. Five negatives in two verses. Paul isn't trying to make us feel bad — he's setting the stage for us to feel the full weight of what God has done. You can't fully appreciate the rescue if you forget what you were rescued from. And you can't fully understand why the New Human Project is so remarkable if you forget the darkness it breaks into. Grace always looks biggest when you hold it next to what it replaced.

## Key Idea

Remembering where we came from keeps us humble, grateful, and full of wonder at what God has done.

## Reflection

What might you start taking for granted about your faith if you forget where you came from?

## Today's Practice

Spend time today in genuine gratitude — not just for what God has given you, but for what he brought you out of.
