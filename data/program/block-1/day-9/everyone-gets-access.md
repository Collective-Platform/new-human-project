## Today's Focus

Through Jesus, every person — regardless of background — comes to God the same way.

## Reading Notes

Through Christ, by the Spirit, both groups now approach the Father. Same door. Same access. No VIP entrance for the people who've grown up in church. No side entrance for the people who feel like they don't deserve to walk straight in. Nobody turned away because of where they come from or what they've done.

In God's new humanity, there are no second-class citizens. Everyone gets in through the same Jesus. The access runs in two directions at once. Vertical, between you and God. And horizontal, between you and the people around you.

And you can't have one without the other. You can't genuinely claim to be close to God while keeping certain people at arm's length. The two are connected. If the cross has torn down the walls between us and God, it's also meant to tear down the walls between us and each other.

That's worth asking yourself honestly: are there walls you're still maintaining that the cross was meant to bring down?

## Key Idea

There are no second-class citizens in God's new humanity. Everyone gets the same access — through the same Jesus.

## Reflection

Is there anyone you've treated — consciously or unconsciously — as less welcome in God's presence than you are?

## Today's Practice

Meditate on the word 'access' today. You have been given an open invitation into the Father's presence. Use it.
