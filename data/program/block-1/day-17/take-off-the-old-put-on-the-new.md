## Today's Focus

Real change starts in the mind — and works its way out into everyday behaviour.

## Reading Notes

Paul describes the old life as one with a darkened, hardened mindset — cut off from God, losing sensitivity to what's right. The new life begins with a renewed mind. We have to think differently before we live differently. Then Paul gets practical with real contrasts: stop lying, tell the truth. Don't let anger fester. Stop taking, start giving. Cut out talk that tears people down, speak words that build people up. Be kind. Forgive. These aren't suggestions for nice people — they are descriptions of what the new humanity actually looks like from Monday to Saturday.

## Key Idea

The new humanity isn't just a Sunday identity. It shows up in how you speak, handle conflict, and treat people every day.

## Reflection

Which of the contrasts Paul lists hits closest to home for you? What's one concrete step toward change?

## Today's Practice

Pick one behaviour from this passage to work on this week. Tell someone you trust so they can help keep you accountable.
