## Today's Focus

Gifts aren't for personal gain, they're for building the whole new humanity up.

## Reading Notes

Christ gave gifts to the church, and those gifts came in the form of people: apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers. But here's the thing most people miss about these roles. Their job isn't to do all the ministry themselves. Their job is to train and equip everyone else to do it.

The new humanity doesn't grow when a few talented people carry the whole load while everyone else watches. It grows when every single member plays their part. Think of it like a body. If only your arms work and everything else just hangs there, something is seriously wrong.

The goal Paul is pointing to is maturity. A community so rooted in truth and love that it doesn't get knocked over every time someone comes along with a convincing new idea or a controversial opinion. Mature communities speak truth, but they do it with love. They build each other up instead of tearing each other down. That's what the New Human Project actually looks like from the inside.

## Key Idea

The new humanity grows when everyone uses their gifts not when a few people do everything.

## Reflection

What gift do you bring to the body of Christ? Are you using it or has it been sitting on the shelf?

## Today's Practice

Tell someone in your church this week what gift you see in them. And ask someone what gift they see in you.
