The New Human Project is inspired by the new life Jesus has won for us on the cross. He came so that we may have life and have it to the full. In this first 25-day block, we will explore from the book of Ephesians what this new life is all about and how we can live it to the fullest, according to God's original intention for all humanity.

**How to Use This Study Plan**

What if Jesus didn't just come to save individuals - but to start something entirely new? Ephesians answers that question. Through the cross, Jesus has launched the New Human Project - a community unlike anything the world has seen, where old divisions are broken down and people who were once strangers become family.

Over 25 days, we'll explore what it means to be part of this new humanity - who we are in Christ, what we've been brought into, and how this changes the way we actually live.

**Each day includes:**

* **Today's Focus -** A single clear aim to keep you anchored as you read.
* **Reading Notes -** Background and insight to help the passage come alive.
* **Key Idea -** One sentence that captures the heart of the day.
* **Reflection -** A question to sit with honestly - alone or in a group.
* **Today's Practice -** Something concrete to do, not just think about.

**Four Movements**

The letter falls into four natural movements. Each builds on the last, moving from what God has done to how we are called to live.

| MOVEMENT 1 | New Life · Days 1-6 God's breathtaking rescue - what he has done for us in Christ, from before creation to our present moment. |  |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **MOVEMENT 2** | **New Society · Days 7-13** The community Christ has created - one new humanity that breaks through every human division. |  |
| **MOVEMENT 3** | **New Standards · Days 14-20** The daily walk of the new humanity - how our identity in Christ reshapes our minds, words, and habits. |  |
| **MOVEMENT 4** | **New Relationships · Days 21-25** The new humanity lived out in every relationship - at home, at work, and in the face of spiritual opposition. |  |
